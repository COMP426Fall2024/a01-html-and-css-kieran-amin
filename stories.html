The Contest for Athens

The first king of Athens, King Cecrops, set out to find a patron deity for his city state. He called upon both Poseidon and Athena, whose intense rivalry nearly led to a war so Athena wisely suggested a peaceful contest instead of a war. The two gods would each give the city a gift and the people would choose the winner. Poseidon struck the ground with his trident and produced a water spring. To the people's dismay, the water was salty as Poseidon channeled this water from the sea. Athena planted an olive tree that grew to provide olives for the city, oil for cooking and lamps, and wood for buildig boats and houses. Thus, Athena was declared the victor and the city was named Athens.

Creating the Horse

The legend states that Poseidon fell in love with the Goddess Demeter and to impress her, he wanted to create the most beautiful creature for her. He worked for some time and was able to create the horse. However, he had taken so long to make the horse, that he was no longer in love with Demeter. He gave the horse to the humans and they were able to use it for racing, transportation and farming.
