Introduction

Poseidon is one of the twelve Olympian gods in Greek mythology. He is the god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses. Poseidon is referenced as one of the Big Three, as he is brothers with Zeus and Hades. Together, the Big Three rule the Heavens, the Earth and Hell.

Mythology

In Greek mythology, Poseidon is often depicted as a powerful and temperamental deity. He is known for his trident, which he uses to control the sea and create earthquakes.

Symbolism

Poseidon is often symbolized by his trident, which represents his power and control over the sea. The trident is also associated with his ability to create earthquakes.
